## My Research Career ##
In the summer of 2017, I joined the Kuhlman Laboratory. My work that year was sponsored by the Philip J. and Betty M. Anthony Undergraduate Research Award.

As an undergraduate researcher, I was fortunate to have a hand in several projects. In my first year with the lab, I conducted experiments to test the retroelement invasion hypothesis in eukaryotes. 

Simply put, this hypothesis posits that incredibly mobile genetic elements, called retroelements, played a major role in the evolution of ancestral eukaryotes. 

A research paper was published in the Proceedings of the National Academy of Sciences (PNAS) in 2018. The paper is available as a PDF at [this link](https://www.pnas.org/content/pnas/115/49/12465.full.pdf).

The Kuhlman Lab was also involved with community outreach. Through the Young Scholars Program, I was a mentor for two local high school students. I had the privelage of teaching these students genetic engineering, microscopy, and wet lab techniques for several weeks.

It was an honor to be a part of the Kuhlman Lab. I made many great friends and the experience cemented my love for applied science.
